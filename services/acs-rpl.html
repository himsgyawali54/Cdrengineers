What is the RPL Report for ACS?

Recognition of Prior Learning (RPL) is the recognition of the skills and experience regarding ICT (Information and Communications Technology) of an applicant by the Australian Computer Society (ACS). The candidate applying for RPL ACS might not have the qualifications to show for his knowledge and capabilities, while migrating to Australia. That is where ACS RPL comes in! It validates the candidates’ experiences and knowledge of ICT declaring him or her as qualified for the related jobs.

Through ACS RPL, anyone, who has been working in the field of ICT, decides to migrate to Australia can be eligible for ICT jobs in Australia too. For this, they must submit a report called RPL Report to ACS. This report helps them demonstrate their knowledge as well as how adept they are at applying that knowledge.

Why is the RPL Report required?

ACS requires proof of the depth of your knowledge and the relevant work experience gained by working in the related field. You must hand in a RPL report for that. It proves that the candidate has the equivalent knowledge and skills to work in ICT professions in Australia too. This report opens a door for migrants to come to Australia under Skilled Migration Visa. That means they can use their years of experience in their field as their qualification when they come to work in Australia. A RPL report for ACS is an integral part of your application. If approved, you can practise as an engineer in Australia too. As the name itself suggests, RPL is a recognition granted to people without ICT qualifications. If they can prove their capabilities, ACS validates their skills and knowledge as a substitute for a degree if their RPL Report gets approval.

What is the format of the RPL report for ACS?

ACS RPL has two sections. They are Key Areas of Knowledge and General ICT Knowledge. In the first section, the applicant is required to demonstrate the knowledge, while in the second section, the applicant needs to show their proficiency in applying that knowledge. The format of RPL report has been laid down below:

Section 1: Key Areas of Knowledge

  • Area A: Essential Core ICT Knowledge
    • Topic 1: ICT Professional Knowledge
    • Topic 2: ICT Problem Solving
  • Area B: General ICT Knowledge
    • Topic 3: Technology Resources
    • Topic 4: Technology Building
    • Topic 5: ICT Management

Section 2: RPL Projects Report

  • Topic 1: Project Summary
  • Topic 2: Business Opportunity or Problem
  • Topic 3: Solutions
  • Topic 4: Results

What are the guidelines for writing an RPL Report for ACS?

These are some of the guidelines for writing RPL report for ACS:

  1. Focus on showing your practical work in real-life situations instead of only theoretical ones.
  2. Don’t exaggerate any claims, which might be detected as falsehood and, thus, be rejected.
  3. Provide references as much as possible. Any information which is not your conception but you are borrowing someone else’s ideas must be referenced, if not it might count as plagiarism.
  4. Never copy and paste any line, no matter how beautifully it has been written. They will detect it right away. Any plagiarism in the report might result in you being banned for a year by their authorities. It must all be in your own words.
  5. Your previous job, which you are using as the basis for RPL report writing, must be listed in ANZSCO.

Requirement for ACS RPL skill assessment

Two projects are required to be submitted for RPL Skill Assessment through ACS Project Report Form. One of the projects should have been undertaken within the last three years, while the other project within the last five years. In the RPL form, they must include their acquired skills for ACS skill assessment. The reports must be filled with details regarding the depth and breadth of ICT knowledge, acquired during the claimed ICT employment. Each report must contain an extensive description of a career episode in the employment history, which provides enough evidence of acs skills and knowledge gained through experience.

Categories for ACS RPL Report Applications

There are two sets of ACS RPL Report Applications. They are:

  1. For applicants without an ICT degree, a minimum of 6 years of professional ICT employment experience in a field, which has an ANZSCO (Australian and New Zealand Standard Classification of Occupations) code, is required.
  2. For applicants without tertiary degrees, an additional two years of employment as ICT skilled workers are necessary (for a total of eight years); however, this extra experience in the field need not have an ANZSCO code.

Frequently Asked Questions

RPL ACS is the Recognition of Prior Learning assessed by the Australian Computer Society. It gives credit to the knowledge and skills of those who have been working in the ICT sector, but are from a non-accord country or whose university is not accredited. It is used to prove that the migrants, who are involved in ICT in their home country, have an equivalent level of knowledge in comparison to the Australian university standards.

The applicant must submit a report to ACS. The first section is Key Areas of Knowledge which includes Essential Core ICT Knowledge, and General ICT Knowledge. The other section is the RPL Project Report. It is a comprehensive report in which you must prove that your skills and knowledge meet the Australian standards. You may have acquired them by studying in college and/or at your workplace.

ACS is a prime body who is responsible for assessing the skills of the migrants, who have applied to work as skilled workers in the ICT (Information and Communication Technology) sector in Australia. It demands a report called the RPL report. It is based on the principle that if the migrants have adequate skills and knowledge, they should be awarded credit for their level of expertise of equivalent qualification to work as skilled workers in Australia.

The ACS assessment processing time takes around 8-10 months to be completed. It may also depend upon the type of ICT job category you are applying for. If additional documents are necessary, it might even take longer.
