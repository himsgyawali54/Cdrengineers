What is the Engineers Australia Skills Assessment?

The Engineers Australia Skills Assessment is a process used to evaluate the qualifications, education, and work experience of engineers who want to migrate to Australia. Engineers Australia is the designated authority responsible for assessing the skills and qualifications of engineers seeking to migrate to Australia under the Skilled Migration Program.

Why Is EA Skills Assessment Required for Migrants?

  • It aims to ensure that international engineers possess the necessary skills and qualifications to work in their chosen occupation in Australia.
  • The assessment process typically involves thoroughly reviewing the applicant's academic and professional qualifications, work experience, and other factors relevant to their engineering field.
  • The Engineers Australia Skills Assessment outcome determines the applicant's eligibility for migration under certain visa categories, such as the Skilled Independent Visa (subclass 189) and the Skilled Nominated Visa (subclass 190).
  • It is important to note that a positive assessment does not guarantee a visa, but it is a crucial step toward achieving that goal.

Who needs Engineers Australia Skills Assessment?

The Engineers Australia Skills Assessment is required for those who wish to migrate to Australia under the skilled migration program and have engineering-related qualifications and experience. It evaluates whether the person's credentials and experience meet the Australian standards for their chosen occupation. Suppose you have engineering or related experience and want to work in Australia. In that case, you will likely need to undergo this assessment to increase the chances of your skills and expertise being recognized and qualifying for the occupation of your choice. Contact us for more detailed information on this.

The skills assessment process for Migrants

Understanding the Criteria

  • Obtain a copy of the Migration Skills Assessment Booklet from Engineers Australia's website.
  • Familiarize yourself with the requirements for your chosen occupation in Australia, including education and work experience.
  • Check if your qualifications are recognized in Australia by visiting the Australian Government's Department of Home Affairs website.
  • Ensure that you meet the English language proficiency requirements for your occupation.

Choosing the Pathway

  • Choose the relevant pathway for your occupation, such as the Professional Engineer, Engineering Technologist, or Engineering Associate pathways.
  • Review the requirements for each pathway to determine the one that matches your education and work experience.

Gathering Relevant Documents

  • Gather all the relevant documents, such as educational qualifications, work experience records, and English language test results.
  • Ensure all the documents are certified copies and translated into English if necessary.

Applying for the Skills Assessment

  • Create an account on Engineers Australia's website and fill in the online application form.
  • Upload all the required documents.
  • Pay the application fee.
  • Wait for Engineers Australia to process your application and provide you with the outcome of your assessment.

Pathways for EA Skill Assessment

Accredited Qualification Pathways

These pathways include:

  • Australian Qualification Pathways
  • Washington Accord Pathways
  • Sydney Accord Pathways
  • Dublin Accord Pathways

Non-Accredited Qualification Pathways

These pathways include:

  • Competency Demonstration Report (CDR)
  • Technical Report
  • Recognition of Prior Learning (RPL)

Checklist for EA Skill Assessment Pathways

Accredited Qualification Pathways

  • Recent passport-size photo
  • Passport
  • Curriculum Vitae
  • Official academic transcript
  • Academic degree qualification
  • Any name change document
  • English language test results (e.g. IELTS, TOEFL)

Non-Accredited Qualification Pathways

  • Recent passport-size photo
  • Passport
  • Curriculum Vitae
  • Official academic transcript
  • Academic degree qualification
  • Any name change document
  • English language test results (e.g. IELTS, TOEFL)
  • Documentary evidence of employment (e.g. employment reference letter and payslips)
  • Relevant registration certificate
  • List of Continuing Professional Development (CPD)
  • Summary Statement
  • Three Career Episodes

Employment Documents

Employment Documents Requirements
Employer Reference Letter
  • Must be on official company letterhead
  • Duration of employment (start date and end date)
  • Job position
  • Duties performed
  • Company name, address, and contact details
  • Signed and dated by an authorized signatory
Continuing Professional Development (CPD) Statement
  • Description of CPD activities undertaken during employment
  • Dates of the CPD activities
  • Verification of the CPD activities
  • Signed and dated by an authorized signatory
Three Career Episodes
  • Details of three significant engineering projects
  • Personal role and responsibilities in each project
  • Evidence of application of engineering knowledge and skills
  • Evidence of achievement of competencies
  • Signed and dated by an authorized signatory
Summary Statement
  • Overview of the career episodes
  • Summary of demonstrated competencies
  • Cross-referencing to the relevant paragraphs in career episodes
  • Signed and dated by an authorized signatory
