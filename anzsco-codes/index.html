As an overseas engineer planning to migrate to Australia, you must be familiar with the Competency Demonstration Report (CDR) that is required for your skilled migration to Australia. A CDR report is a crucial document that engineers need to submit to Engineers Australia to showcase their qualifications, experience, and competencies to excel in their chosen engineering domain. The selected candidates obtain skilled visas to Australia.

There are a series of aspects that play significant roles in the assessment of your skilled migration application. One such aspect is the ANZSCO code.

What is ANZSCO?

ANZSCO stands for Australian and New Zealand Standard Classification of Occupations and it is a system used to classify jobs and occupations in Australia and New Zealand. Itâ€™s a joint initiative between the Australian Bureau of Statistics (ABS), Statistics New Zealand, and the Department of Jobs and Small Business. ANZSCO codes are crucial in the migration process, as they help determine that you are eligible to work as an engineer in Australia in your chosen discipline. Understanding ANZSCO Skill Level

ANZSCO Skill Level is a classification that reflects the amount and type of training, education, and experience required to perform the tasks and duties of a specific engineering discipline competently. Skill levels range from 1 to 5, with 1 being the highest level requiring the most expertise.

  • Skill Level 1: It corresponds to a bachelor's degree or higher qualification, or at least 5 years of relevant experience. In some instances, a combination of both can be required.
  • Skill Level 2: A Skill Level 2 occupation requires an AQF Associate Degree, Advanced Diploma, or Diploma, or at least three years of relevant experience.
  • Skill Level 3: For Skill Level 3, an AQF Certificate IV or AQF Certificate III, including at least two years of on-the-job training, or at least three years of relevant experience is necessary.
  • Skill Level 4: This level corresponds to an AQF Certificate II or III, or at least one year of relevant experience.
  • Skill Level 5: Skill Level 5 occupations require an AQF Certificate I, or compulsory secondary education, or at the very least, some short-term on-the-job training.

How to Choose the Right ANZSCO Code for CDR?

It is crucial to understand the roles and responsibilities for your nominated engineering domain to choose the right ANZSCO code for your CDR report. You must perform the ANZSCO codes search thoroughly and understand the roles, tasks, and responsibilities associated with it. Your CDR report should align with your nominated ANZSCO code.

Tips for Aligning Your CDR with ANZSCO

You can immensely increase your chances of a successful skill assessment by aligning your CDR report with your nominated ANZSCO code. Below are the tips on how you can align your CDR with ANZSCO.

  • Research your ANZSCO code thoroughly and understand the roles, tasks, and responsibilities associated with it.
  • Ensure that your career episodes demonstrate your skills and qualifications in the context of your ANZSCO code.
  • Use relevant engineering terminologies and concepts that align with your ANZSCO code to showcase your expertise in the field.
  • Provide evidence of how your work experience and projects have contributed to meeting the competency requirements of your nominated occupation.
  • Highlight your achievements and accomplishments that demonstrate your ability to excel in your nominated occupation as per the ANZSCO code.

Importance of ANZSCO Codes in CDR

An ANZSCO code in your CDR Report is important for a successful Migration Skills Assessment. It allows Engineers Australia to assess your engineering competencies and verify that your skills and qualifications align with the nominated occupation. You can go through the ANZSCO code list and determine the ANZSCO code that fits your chosen engineering domain.
